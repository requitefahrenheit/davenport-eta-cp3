## THE GRAIL (Open Problem)

**Conjecture**: η(C_p^{r+1}) = (2α(r)+1)(p−1)+1 where α(r) = 9·2^{r−3}−1 for r ≥ 3.

Equivalently: among 2α(r)+2 nonzero non-proportional vectors in F_p^{r+1}, there exists a weighted zero-sum of weight ≤ p.

Base case (r=3): 18 vectors in F_p⁴.

### Current Best Proof Strategy: Two-Case Split

Discovered by the adversary agent (cc-collab, 2026-03-19):

**Case 1 — Weakly ZSF**: If the set S has an internal zero-sum at weight exactly p, the grail holds trivially. Computational evidence: ALL ZSF sets of size 17 except doubled+e₁ are weakly ZSF.

**Case 2 — Strongly ZSF**: No zero-sum at weight ≤ p at all. Appears to be UNIQUE — only the doubled+e₁ construction. Shadow Saturation verified at p=5,7,11,13,17,19,23 (shadow at weight p−1 covers all p⁴−1 nonzero vectors).

**What needs proving**:
- (a) Doubled+e₁ is the unique strongly-ZSF set of size 2α(r)+1
- (b) Shadow saturation for doubled+e₁ at all p

### Confirmed Dead Ends
- Character theory for support ≥ 4 (W(χ)=0 for even characters)
- k=4 parity theorem (WRONG)
- Classical Chevalley-Warning (fires only p=5, 2-3 free vars always)
- Grail Lemma N_p ≡ k mod p (FALSE for ZSF sequences)
- Weight-4p → weight-p decomposition (FAILS)
- Shadow Saturation for ALL ZSF sets (FALSE — cap counterexample at p=7)

### Alternate Strategy: Projection + Weight Control
Project 18 vectors to F_p³ by quotienting out v_k. Copies pool exceeds η(C_p³), get zero-sum of length ≤ p. Lift back: residual μ·v_k. Gap: one inequality (a_k ≥ μ).
