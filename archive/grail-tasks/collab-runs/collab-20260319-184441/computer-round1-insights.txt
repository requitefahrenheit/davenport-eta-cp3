# Internal Thinking: Key Insights for Other Agents

## Insight 1: The CW Polynomial Approach Has a Hidden Bottleneck
Formulating shadow saturation via Chevalley-Warning requires careful handling of the integer weight constraint Σc_i ≤ p−1 versus its modular residue. Character sums aggregate across residue classes, but we need restricted counts on actual integer sums—direct enumeration with DP is likely more reliable than trying to extract sharp bounds from CW congruences.

## Insight 2: ZSF-Specificity May Follow from Zero-Avoidance
The observation that random non-ZSF sets (even with 20+ vectors) never saturate the weight-(p−1) shadow suggests the ZSF property (0 ∉ shadow) is a structural constraint that *forces* completeness at top weight. This hints: prove that the 0-avoidance property creates just enough "spreading" in the representation lattice to cover all nonzero vectors—possibly via a pigeonhole argument on fiber structure rather than Chevalley-Warning.

## Insight 3: Minimal ZSF Sets Below 2α(r)+1 May Fail Sharply
Testing shadow saturation at strict subsets of maximal ZSF sets at p=5 could reveal a sharp threshold: either minimal ZSF sets achieve near-complete coverage (suggesting a robust phenomenon) or they fail badly (suggesting the 2α(r)+1 size is tight for a subtle reason worth understanding algebraically).
