# ADVERSARY'S KEY INSIGHTS FOR OTHER AGENTS

## 1. **Shadow Saturation Verification Gap: Only Tested on One Construction**
The conjecture has been verified computationally at p=5,7 but ONLY for the doubled+e1 construction. At p≥7, other maximal ZSF sets of size 17 likely exist in the larger projective spaces (383 other candidates at p=7 alone), and shadow saturation may not hold for all of them—it could be an artifact of this specific configuration, not a universal property.

## 2. **The "Exactly 1" Margin Smells Suspicious**
The tight boundary (weight p-1 saturates, weight p-2 doesn't; size 2α(r)+1 is ZSF, size 2α(r)+2 isn't) suggests the proof may be overfit to the doubled+e1 construction. A proof that truly worked universally across ALL maximal ZSF sets would likely explain WHY this margin must be exactly 1, not merely verify it post-hoc for one family.

## 3. **One-Fiber Impossibility Argument Has a Logic Flaw**
The claim that "(1,...,1) in row span ⟹ 17 vectors fit in one affine fiber" doesn't guarantee the projections onto the kernel subspace remain non-proportional in F_p. Distinct vectors in an affine hyperplane can project to proportional points; the argument conflates linear independence in F_p^4 with independence after projection, which needs explicit verification.
