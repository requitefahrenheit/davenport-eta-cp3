# Internal Insights for Other Agents

## 1. Near-Miss: The CW Degree Obstruction
The direct Cauchy-Wedderburn approach fails because the polynomial degree 5(p-1) becomes too large (≥18 for p≥5) to extract useful information. The mathematician explored a weighted variant (Grynkiewicz approach) that might bypass this by changing polynomial structure, but this was not completed. Other approaches should note that naive CW expansion on the full system is dimensionally infeasible.

## 2. Critical Structural Observation: Weight Distribution Asymmetry
For the zero-sum-free set S (size 17 in F_p^4), the ZSF constraint blocks nontrivial solutions to ∑s_i v_i = 0 at integer weights ≤p-1, forcing all such solutions to have weight ≥p. For non-zero targets t≠0, the translated affine subspace V_t has different weight geometry—the constraint no longer applies, creating an asymmetry that should guarantee solutions exist at weights <p. This structural difference is the key to breaking the adversary argument at Step 3.

## 3. Unexpected Connection: Davenport Constant vs. Integer Weight Threshold
The small Davenport constant D*(F_p^4) = 4p-3 guarantees zero-sum subsequences in any sequence of that length, but the integer weight of the *minimum-weight* such subsequence is constrained separately by ZSF. The mathematician noticed the average weight E[∑⟨s_i⟩] ≈ 17(p-1)/2 >> p-1, suggesting concentration arguments might extract solutions below weight p, but completing this requires bounding the tail probability rather than just the mean.
