# Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Multi-fiber ZSF sets may not exist at small primes**
The existing verification only confirms that the standard two-fiber construction cannot be extended beyond 17 vectors at p=5 (all 607 18th-vector candidates killed). Exhaustive enumeration of *all* 18-element subsets is combinatorially prohibitive, but critically: **we haven't verified that any distinct maximal ZSF set of size 17 exists in F_p^4**—only that the doubled+e₁ construction is maximal. If this construction is unique (up to equivalence), then the two-fiber bound alone suffices for the proof, eliminating the multi-fiber gap entirely.

## 2. **Support-based kill mechanisms have clear structure that scales to p=7**
At p=5, 513 of 607 candidate 18th vectors are killed by support-3 zero-sums (collinear triples) and 94 by support-4 four-element configurations. **These mechanisms depend only on the rank structure and linear dependence relations, not on the prime size itself**—suggesting the same patterns should persist at p=7. Verifying whether all remaining ~383 vectors at p=7 are killed by the same structural constraints would confirm the approach generalizes.

## 3. **Dynamic programming on cumulative sums is computationally tractable for verification**
Rather than enumerate all 18-element subsets (intractable), testing whether a given 18-vector configuration admits a weighted zero-sum with weight ≤ p can be done in O(n · p^d · p) time via DP on vector sums mod p. This makes systematic verification at p=7 feasible and could distinguish between configurations requiring support-3 vs. support-4 mechanisms.
