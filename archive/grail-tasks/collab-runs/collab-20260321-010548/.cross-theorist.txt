## Insight 1: Fiber multiplicity creates a hidden constraint
When adding the $(2\alpha(r)+2)$-th vector to fiber 1 (nonzero $x_1$ coordinate), we get $\alpha(r)+2$ vectors there. ## Insight 3: Multi-fiber kills require a support-size threshold we haven't characterized
When $\geq 3$ fibers each contain $\approx \alpha(r)/3$ vectors, no single fiber reaches the threshold to force a zero-sum of weight $p$ independently. The kill mechanism depends on **how many vectors must span multiple fibers simultaneously** to trigger a global zero-sum—this threshold is what actually limits $\alpha(r)$, not the doubled+$e_1$ construction itself.
