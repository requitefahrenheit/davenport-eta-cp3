### Three Critical Discoveries

**1. Support-5 first appears at p=11.** Eight candidates require support-5 zero-sums. These have:
- **ZERO** collinear triples with construction vectors (not in any 2D subspace spanned by construction pairs)
- ~59 dependent quadruples, but **NONE** with weight-≤p positive kernel vectors
- Support-5 kills ALL have weight exactly p = 11
- They come in 4 symmetric pairs under coordinate swap: (1,0,3,8)↔(1,0,8,3), etc.

**2. Support-3 kill rate is strictly decreasing:** 79.9% → 53.3% → 28.2% → 21.8%. Matches the non-degeneracy formula. The rank-3 theorem handles an ever-shrinking fraction at larger primes.

**3. Weight concentrates at w = p:** 92-95% of ALL kills have weight exactly p. The bound is maximally tight.

### Implications for the Proof Strategy

- **Cannot rely on support-3 alone.** The rank-3 subroutine becomes minority at p ≥ 11.
- **Cannot rely on support ≤ 4.** Support-5 is genuinely needed at p ≥ 11.
- **Must handle arbitrary support.** The maximum support likely grows (slowly) with p.
- **Weight is always exactly p** for the killing zero-sum. The "copies pool" formulation (find a length-≤p zero-sum subsequence) is the right framework.
- **The inductive approach must be support-agnostic**: any proof by induction on rank must produce zero-sums without constraining support size.
