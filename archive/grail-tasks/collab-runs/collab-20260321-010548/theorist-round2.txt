# THEORIST ROUND 2 — RESULTS

## Major New Results

### 1. Universal Shadow Saturation (PROVED — 5-line proof)

**The key theorem of this round.** For ANY strongly ZSF α(r)-set B in F_p^r (not just A₈):

> Σ_{≤p-1}(B) = F_p^r \ {0}

**Proof sketch:** To show v ∈ shadow, add ONE copy of −v to B^{p−1}, reaching exactly η(C_p^r) elements. The η bound forces a zero-sum. If it doesn't use −v, it contradicts ZSF. If it does (with coefficient t=1), we get v = Σc_i b_i at weight ≤ p−1 directly.

**Key innovation:** Prior "three-line argument" (flagged by Adversary) used −v with multiplicity p−1, yielding s₉v (not v) in the shadow. My proof uses multiplicity **1**, so t ∈ {0,1} — no rescaling bug.

This **resolves Adversary Attacks 2 and 3**: shadow saturation is universal across ALL GL classes, proved from η alone, requiring zero structural information about B.

### 2. Weight Flexibility Theorem is FALSE

The approach from `goal.md` (Cauchy-Davenport on weight sets from different fibers) **cannot work**. Counterexample: A₈ ∪ {(0,1,1)} at p=5 has only **one** achievable zero-sum weight (w=p=5), far below the needed (p+1)/2 = 3.

### 3. Weight Mod-p Invariance (PROVED)

Zero-sum-preserving perturbations (same tail, same weight mod p) change the integer weight by **multiples of p only**. This explains why weights cluster at exactly p.

### 4. Precise Inductive Bottleneck Identified

The inductive step works when:
- Fiber 0 has ≥ α(r)+1 vectors (η bound, weight ≤ p, lifts trivially)
- Any nonzero fiber has ≥ α(r)+2 vectors (s bound gives weight exactly p)

It **fails** when:
- Each nonzero fiber has ≤ α(r)+1 vectors (= exactly s−1 copies, one short of the s threshold)
- The η bound gives weight ≤ p but NOT weight = p specifically
- Lifting requires weight ≡ 0 (mod p), i.e., weight = p exactly

### 5. The ONE Remaining Gap

**Cross-Fiber Solvability:** Can partial sums from multiple fibers be combined to satisfy both the tail equation AND the first-coordinate constraint? This requires matching **exact-weight** representations, not just cumulative shadows.
