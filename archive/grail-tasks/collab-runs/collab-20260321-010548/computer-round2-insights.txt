# Mathematician's Internal Thinking: Key Insights for Round 2

## Insight 1: Fiber multiplicity creates a hidden constraint
When adding the $(2\alpha(r)+2)$-th vector to fiber 1 (nonzero $x_1$ coordinate), we get $\alpha(r)+2$ vectors there. A zero-sum among them **only lifts globally if weight is divisible by p**, since all $x_1$-coordinates equal 1. This forces us to need the $s$-constant (weight exactly $p$), not $\eta$-constant (any zero-sum)—the induction argument breaks unless we can guarantee both the zero-sum existence AND its weight being a multiple of $p$ simultaneously.

## Insight 2: Extremal uniqueness verification is tractable via neighbor enumeration
Rather than exhaustively enumerate all 17-element ZSF sets in $F_5^4$ (prohibitively large), swap individual vectors from the known doubled+$e_1$ construction with candidates from the 139 "killable" 18th vectors to generate neighboring maximal ZSF sets. If no swaps preserve ZSF-ness, doubled+$e_1$ is structurally isolated, validating uniqueness claims.

## Insight 3: Multi-fiber kills require a support-size threshold we haven't characterized
When $\geq 3$ fibers each contain $\approx \alpha(r)/3$ vectors, no single fiber reaches the threshold to force a zero-sum of weight $p$ independently. The kill mechanism depends on **how many vectors must span multiple fibers simultaneously** to trigger a global zero-sum—this threshold is what actually limits $\alpha(r)$, not the doubled+$e_1$ construction itself.
