# Three Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Support-4 Zero-Sums Hit a Hard Ceiling at p ≥ 17**
The lift argument from rank-3 fails because support-4 zero-sums can achieve at most 16 distinct first-coordinate targets—insufficient to cover all residues when p ≥ 17. This suggests the 8-tail bound requires a fundamentally different mechanism, not just optimization of the existing lift. Consider whether multi-support configurations or hybrid strategies are necessary here.

## 2. **Fiber Proportionality Creates a Subtle Obstruction in Inductive Scaling**
When projecting vectors to fibers in F_p^r, non-proportionality in F_p^{r+1} doesn't guarantee non-proportionality of tail vectors in F_p^r, blocking direct application of the inductive η bound. The fiber-0 case (where tails are equivalent to full vectors) and fiber-c ≠ 0 cases behave categorically differently—proportional tails in fiber-c can only occur if coefficients align in a highly constrained way.

## 3. **Weight Polytope Achievement May Fail on Intervals Over F_p**
The achievable zero-sum weights form a linear image of a solution lattice in (Z/pZ)^n, but contiguity of integer weights in the achieved interval is not guaranteed by convexity alone—the modular constraint and discrete coefficient domain create gaps. This suggests the Weight Flexibility Theorem needs a number-theoretic proof via Cauchy-Davenport or character sums, not just convex geometry.
