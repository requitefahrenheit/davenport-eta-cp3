# Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Circular Reasoning Trap in Shadow Saturation**
The shadow saturation proof for η(C_p^r) assumes the η formula already holds, which blocks direct proof by induction. The actual leverage comes from the *base case* (r=3, verified in Lean) and fiber projections, not from assuming the formula inductively—this suggests the proof structure must route through explicit fiber-by-fiber analysis rather than recursive application.

## 2. **Fiber Weight Divisibility Breaks Down Before Rank 5**
When a fiber at coordinate c has ≥α(r)+2 vectors, zero-sum weights within that fiber are severely restricted (divisible by p in specific ways), but this bottleneck *fails at p=3* and becomes loose at higher ranks. The weight-p barrier only cleanly enforces unique achievable weights for special fiber configurations, not universally—other approaches should exploit which fiber sizes actually trigger the divisibility constraint.

## 3. **Projection Strategy Requires Explicit Fiber Enumeration**
Linear functional projections splitting α(r)+1 vectors into fibers don't automatically recover the η bound; the proof must track which fiber configurations can coexist and verify that *no arrangement avoids a weight-p zero-sum*. The "doubled+e1" construction shows one failure mode, but generalizing requires exhaustively ruling out other multi-fiber arrangements—this is computational-bottleneck territory, not abstract.
