## CONVERGENCE/ESCALATION

### HIGH CONFIDENCE — Shadow Saturation Theorem (Universal)
**THEORIST** and **COMPUTER** converged independently on: For ANY strongly ZSF α(r)-set B in F_p^r,
```
Σ_{≤p-1}(B) = F_p^r \ {0}
```
- **THEORIST**: 5-line proof using η bound with coefficient multiplicity t=1 (fixing prior t=p−1 bug)
- **COMPUTER**: Empirical verification across rank-4, p=5 configurations
- **Status**: Resolves ADVERSARY Attacks 2–3 on shadow saturation universality

### 🚩 POSSIBLE FALSE CONJECTURE — Two-Fiber Decomposition as Universal Property
**THEORIST** and **COMPUTER** independently converged on the **same failure mechanism**:
- **THEORIST**: Induction fails when "each nonzero fiber has ≤ α(r)+1 vectors (= exactly s−1 copies, one short of the s threshold)"
- **COMPUTER**: "2-fiber result was an artifact... normalized vectors trivially satisfy condition... 2-fiber theorem alone doesn't suffice"
- **Joint Diagnosis**: Fiber-balance constraints are **category errors**—fibers 0 and c≠0 have fundamentally different zero-sum mechanics (tails proportional in fiber-0 directly; proportional tails in fiber-c require aligned coefficients)

### 🚩 CRITICAL SCOPE ERROR — Grail Formula Invalid at p=3
**ADVERSARY** identified explicit contradiction:
- Formula claims: η(C_3^4) ≤ 9·2¹−1 = **17** (for "all odd primes p")
- Reality: α(4,3) ≥ **19** via cap set theory (Hill cap has 20 points in PG(3,3))
- Mechanism: At p=3, ZSF ≡ cap set (no 3-term zero-sums); cap sets grow faster than formula predicts
- **Conclusion**: Formula requires **p ≥ 5 restriction**; all claims using "all odd primes" are vacuously invalid

### 🚩 POSSIBLE FALSE CONJECTURE — Projection Lifting Completeness
**ADVERSARY** identified arithmetic gap that **neither THEORIST nor COMPUTER addressed**:
- Quotient zero-sum Σs_i·π(v_i) = 0 lifts to Σs_i·v_i = c·v_k with c ≠ 0
- To close globally requires s_k = p−c, but completing weight = (quotient weight) + (p−c)
- **Unresolved**: Inductive hypothesis only guarantees quotient weight ≤ some bound; cannot guarantee quotient weight ≤ c for arbitrary c
- **Impact**: Lifting strategy may be incomplete for general rank-r induction

## CROSS-POLLINATION: Synthesized Insights

### From THEORIST → COMPUTER & ADVERSARY
1. **Weight Mod-p Invariance** (proved): Zero-sum perturbations change integer weight by multiples of p only — explains why weights cluster at exactly p. This suggests fiber-weight constraints are not just structural but **number-theoretic**.
2. **Universal Shadow via η alone** (proved): No structural knowledge of B needed. Implication: proof should work mod-p theoretically without requiring special constructions.

### From COMPUTER → THEORIST & ADVERSARY
1. **Fiber multiplicity creates hidden constraints**: When fiber 1 reaches α(r)+2 vectors, ALL zero-sums among them have coordinates summing to 1(α(r)+2) = 1 (mod p), forcing weight divisibility check—the induction needs **simultaneous existence AND weight-divisibility**, not just existence.
2. **Extremal uniqueness is verifiable via neighbor swaps**: Rather than exhaustive enumeration, test whether the doubled+e₁ construction is structurally isolated by swap-preservation; if all 139 candidate swaps break ZSF-ness, the construction is proven extremal.

### From ADVERSARY → THEORIST & COMPUTER
1. **p=3 is a hard boundary**: The formula fails at p=3 due to cap set growth rate. This suggests the true bottleneck is **p-dependent**, not just rank-dependent—proofs must stratify by p.
2. **Double-swap behavior varies non-monotonically**: At p=5, all 128 double swaps preserve ZSF; at p=7, weight capacity increases, allowing new fiber compositions. Proof techniques cannot assume monotonicity in p.
3. **Weight Flexibility needs circular strengthening**: Cannot use "many achievable weights" to apply Cauchy-Davenport without first proving the many-weights property—hidden dependency in proof chain.
