# Mathematician's Internal Analysis – Round 3 Critical Insights

## Key Near-Misses & Structural Observations

**Insight 1: Recursion Consistency Breakdown Below Rank 3**
The formula α(r+1) = 2α(r)+1 works perfectly for r ≥ 3 (giving α(3)=8, α(4)=17), but the derivation collapses at r=1,2 where it produces non-integers or contradicts direct Davenport-constant calculations. This suggests the fiber-balance constraint may be a *rank-≥3 phenomenon*, not a universal property—the extremal structure at lower dimensions might obey different logic entirely.

**Insight 2: Fiber-Balance Independence from Coordinate Choice**
The construction B (doubled+e₁) and its coordinate-permuted variant B' encode the *same* ZSF set up to relabeling. Both admit 2-fiber projections, implying the extremal ZSF structure *forces* multi-fiber decomposability across some coordinate choice, rather than being projection-dependent. This is the structural rigidity the induction relies on—but it remains unproven that ALL maximal rank-4 sets share this property.
