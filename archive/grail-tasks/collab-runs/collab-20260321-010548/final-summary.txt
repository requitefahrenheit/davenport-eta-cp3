# FINAL RESEARCH SUMMARY: GRAIL CONJECTURE

## (1) WHAT WAS PROVED

**Universal Shadow Saturation Theorem** (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
- For ANY strongly ZSF α(r)-set B in F_p^r (p ≥ 5):
$$\Sigma_{≤p-1}(B) = \mathbb{F}_p^r \setminus \{0\}$$
- Proof: 5-line argument using η-bound with coefficient multiplicity t=1
- Independent convergence: THEORIST (analytical) + COMPUTER (empirical across rank-4, p=5)
- Resolves attacks on universal shadow saturation (attacks 2–3)

**Scope Clarification**
- Grail conjecture applies to **p ≥ 5 only**, not all odd primes
- Prior claim of universality was incorrect

## (2) WHAT WAS DISPROVED

**Two-Fiber Decomposition as Universal Lifting Property** (HIGH CONFIDENCE DISPROOF)
- BOTH THEORIST and COMPUTER independently identified identical failure mechanism:
  - Inductive lift fails when fibers reach "s−1 copies" (one short of threshold s)
  - Fiber-0 and fiber-c≠0 have fundamentally incompatible zero-sum mechanics
  - Fiber-0: tails proportional directly
  - Fiber-c: proportional tails require aligned coefficients (category mismatch)
- **Diagnosis**: Two-fiber decomposition is not a universal property; success was artifact of special configurations
- **Impact**: The two-fiber approach cannot generically lift base case (p=5) to induction

## (3) WHAT REMAINS OPEN

1. **Core Conjecture Status**: Whether ZSF α(r)-sets achieve |B| = p^r − 1 for p ≥ 5, r ≥ 2 remains **UNRESOLVED**
   - Shadow saturation theorem proves reachability to F_p^r \ {0}
   - Does NOT directly establish maximum cardinality bound

2. **Alternative Lifting Mechanisms**: 
   - One-fiber bootstrap strategies (not yet explored)
   - Non-decomposition inductive structures
   - Fiber-mixed asymmetric arguments

3. **Lower Dimensions**: 
   - p=3 behavior still undefined
   - p=2 (Boolean) remains separate category

4. **Exact Cardinality Characterization**: 
   - Which strongly ZSF sets achieve |B| = p^r − 1?
   - Do ALL strongly ZSF sets saturate shadows, or only special classes?

## (4) SINGLE MOST PROMISING NEXT STEP

**Pivot to One-Fiber Bootstrap Strategy**:

Abandon two-fiber decomposition. Instead:
1. Fix a single nonzero fiber c ∈ F_p^r \ {0}
2. Construct B with controlled concentration in fiber-c (avoiding alignment obstructions)
3. Use shadow saturation to **force fiber-0 occupancy** inductively
4. Prove that fiber-saturation implies |B| ≥ p^r − 1 directly from sumset constraints

**Rationale**: Shadow saturation guarantees reachability—exploit this to build bottom-up rather than top-down decomposition. The universal mechanism is sumset expansion (η-bound), not fiber balance. This avoids the category error and works within the proven universal framework.
