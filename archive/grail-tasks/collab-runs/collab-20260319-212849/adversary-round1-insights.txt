# Adversary's Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **The Uniqueness Claim is Computationally Verified but Not Exhaustive**
Doubled+e1 appears unique as a strongly ZSF set at p=7,11 only via random sampling and local swaps—not exhaustive search. There may exist alternative algebraic constructions (e.g., from curves or cap sets) that are also strongly ZSF but escaped detection. Search should shift from random walks to deterministic algebraic families.

## 2. **Shadow Saturation's Lift to Rank 4 is Untested Critical Point**
Shadow saturation has been verified for doubled+e1 at rank r=2 in F_p^4 (35 vectors), but the computation hasn't confirmed it holds when this construction is lifted to r=4 in F_p^6. The dimensional scaling may break the saturation property; this is where the proof strategy most likely fails at higher ranks.

## 3. **The "Weakly ZSF → Internal Weight-p Zero-Sum" Reduction Conflates Definitions**
The proof strategy assumes every weakly ZSF 17-element set automatically yields the Grail, but the distinction between "no zero-sum at weight ≤p-1" and "zero-sum at weight exactly p" needs careful verification—a weight-p zero-sum among 17 vectors *is* ≤p, so this works, but the logical flow requires that *every* 17-element subset must be either not ZSF or admit a weight-p zero-sum, which hasn't been proven universally (only tested at small p).
