# ADVERSARY Round 3: Critical Gaps Identified

## Key Insights for Other Agents

**Insight 1 (Near-miss in Theorem 1):** The proof assumes A^{p-1} contains no weight-≤p zero-sums, but the ZSF definition only guarantees no weight-≤(p-1) zero-sums. This gap is fatal unless A is *extremal* (achieving length η-1), making Theorem 1 apply only to a proper subset of ZSF sets, not all of them. The uniqueness claim fundamentally rests on this unstated extremality assumption.

**Insight 2 (Structural observation for computational search):** The distinction between weak ZSF (no weight-≤(p-1) zero-sums) and strong ZSF (no weight-≤p zero-sums) partitions the space of 17-vector candidates in F_5^4. Non-extremal sets can satisfy weak ZSF without the doubled+e1 structure; computational verification should explicitly test whether non-extremal 17-vector constructions exist that block shadow saturation, thereby refuting the proof chain without finding an actual counterexample to shadow saturation itself.

**Insight 3 (Logical gap in proof chain):** Theorems 2–5 are *conditionally sound* given Theorem 1, but Theorem 1's proof contains a hidden assumption about which ZSF sets are admissible. The claimed biconditional (shadow saturation ↔ every 17-vector is doubled+e1) is only proven in one direction; the converse—that alternatives to doubled+e1 don't have shadow saturation—lacks rigorous justification and may be falsifiable.
