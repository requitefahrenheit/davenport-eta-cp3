**2. Weight-p zero-sum threshold is fragile; first counterexample would collapse "strongly ZSF" classification.**
I've verified the first internal zero-sum (at weight ≤p, not projective) appears at weight p+1 for all tested p. If this fails at any prime (especially p≥29 where I haven't checked), the dichotomy between weakly/strongly ZSF collapses, invalidating the entire construction strategy.

**3. Shadow saturation at r=5 untested — counts suggest it should hold (E[misses]≈0.27 at r=4,p=5), but this is extrapolation.**
The r=4 verification at p=5 works because 35 vectors generate 79 shadow elements covering 80 targets with high probability. At r=5 (71 vectors in F_5^6), the combinatorial structure may degrade; collision probabilities shift unpredictably.
