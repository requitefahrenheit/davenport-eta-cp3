# Mathematician's Internal Thinking: Key Insights

## Insight 1: Near-miss with direct Davenport constant application
The copies pool construction (taking each of 2α(r)+1 vectors with multiplicity p-1) yields (2α(r)+1)(p-1) elements, vastly exceeding D(C_p^{r+1}). However, this guarantees only *existence* of a zero-sum subsequence of length ≤ p, not that the shadow reaches every nonzero vector—coverage requires showing the shadow is *universal*, not just that it contains one zero-sum, making simple subsequence-counting arguments insufficient.

## Insight 2: Structural bottleneck in additive combinatorics bounds
Kneser's theorem gives |kS| ≥ 17k - k + 1 for sumsets in vector spaces, but this linear growth saturates at k ≈ 37 to cover p^4 elements—far exceeding the weight bound of p-1 ≈ 4. The resolution likely requires that **non-proportionality of vectors in F_p^{r+1} forces superlinear sumset expansion** (multiplicative rather than additive), a special property not captured by generic Kneser bounds.

## Insight 3: ZSF condition encodes a dual congruence constraint
A set S is ZSF if no nontrivial solution exists to Σs_i v_i = 0 with s_i ∈ {0,...,p-1} and total weight ≤ p-1. The boundary between weight ≤ p-1 (allowed in shadow) and weight = p (excluded) is critical—proving shadow saturation likely requires showing the weight-p threshold is forced precisely by counting constraints on the dual lattice of representations.
