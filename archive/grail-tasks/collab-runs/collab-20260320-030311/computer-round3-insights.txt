# Mathematician's Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Near-miss: Brute-force coefficient enumeration fails at scale**
Exhaustively checking all p^8 coefficient combinations for A8's zero-sums is feasible for small p (p≤7), but p=11 requires 214M operations and becomes intractable. The insight: iterating by *weight* rather than coefficients (i.e., loop over weight w=1…p, then find if any coefficient vector summing to w produces zero) reduces the search space dramatically and should be adopted by follow-up work.

## 2. **Structural observation: Shadow saturation robustness across non-unique bases**
Although ~640K alternative 8-element strongly ZSF sets exist in F_5^3, Cortex confirmed ALL achieve full shadow saturation when doubled+augmented with e_1 in F_5^4. This suggests saturation is a property of the *construction topology* (2α(r)+1 vector architecture), not the specific base set—other approaches should exploit this invariance rather than search for unique configurations.

## 3. **Unexpected connection: Rigidity vs. saturation are decoupled**
S17 exhibits rigidity (no single-swap preserves strongly ZSF at p=5,7) yet shadow saturation holds robustly. Rigidity is a *local* property (perturbations fail), while saturation is a *global* property (weight-p coverage); these are not contradictory—one constrains alternatives, the other constrains reachability. Proof strategies should treat them as independent verification axes.
