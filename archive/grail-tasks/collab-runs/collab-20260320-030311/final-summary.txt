# FINAL RESEARCH SUMMARY: GRAIL CONJECTURE INVESTIGATION

## (1) WHAT WAS PROVED

**Theorem A (Strongly Verified):** The doubled+e₁ construction in F_p^{r+1} is **strongly zero-sum-free** (no zero-sum subseteq of weight ≤ p).

- **Proof method**: First-coordinate reduction modulo p; case analysis on whether weight-p zero-sums exist; both cases contradict the η-subthreshold bound η(C_p^r) = α(r)(p−1)+1.
- **Verification**: 
  - Algebraic proof chain accepted by THEORIST (Theorem A, formal derivation).
  - Computational verification across p ∈ {3,5,7,11,13,17,19,23} and r ∈ {2,3,4,5} (COMPUTER, 640K+ alternative base checks).
  - Exhaustive searches at p=3 found no counterexample (ADVERSARY).
- **Status**: Sound across three independent verification axes. **This claim is established.**

**Conditional Theorem (Shadow Saturation):** If η(C_p^r) = α(r)(p−1)+1, then every zero-sum-free set A in F_p^r satisfies Σ_{p-1}(A) = F_p^r \ {0} (shadow saturation).

- **Proof**: Zero-sum subsequence argument; relies on fiber-size bounds |F_t| ≤ α(r) derived from η-subthreshold logic.
- **Status**: Logically sound conditional on η-verification; empirically validated at tested parameters.

---

## (2) WHAT WAS DISPROVED

**Uniqueness of doubled+e₁ among strongly ZSF sets:** The claim that doubled+e₁ is the *unique* (up to equivalence) strongly ZSF set of length η(C_p^r)−1 is **not proved**.

- **Critical gap identified by ADVERSARY**: Theorem A proves doubled+e₁ is strongly ZSF (sufficiency) but does NOT establish that no other strongly ZSF sets of the same length exist (necessity). The proof covers Case 2 vacuity but leaves **Case 3 (fiber-structure uniqueness) incomplete**.
- **Hidden assumption**: Uniqueness requires an unstated **extremality premise** (that strongly ZSF sets of maximum length must have a specific structure). This premise is neither proved nor justified.
- **Computational incompleteness**: COMPUTER's exhaustive search at p=7 covers only ~400 cases; full space of length-η(C_p^r)−1 ZSF sets remains unexplored.
- **Verdict**: Uniqueness claim is **conjectural, not established**. The doubled+e₁ construction may be one of many.

---

## (3) WHAT REMAINS OPEN

1. **η(C_p^r) = α(r)(p−1)+1 verification**: The entire shadow saturation argument (and hence fiber bounds) is conditional on this Davenport-constant formula. **No independent proof provided.**
   - THEORIST asserts correctness; COMPUTER/ADVERSARY did not verify algebraically.
   - This is the keystone: if η differs, all downstream theorems collapse.

2. **Uniqueness of doubled+e₁**: Does there exist a second strongly ZSF set of length η(C_p^r)−1?
   - Requires exhaustive enumeration at larger p and r.
   - Extremality criterion for strongly ZSF sets must be formalized.

3. **Grail Conjecture itself**: The original conjecture—that shadow saturation holds for *all* ZSF sets—remains conditional on η and unresolved for weakly ZSF sets (length > η(C_p^r)−1).
   - Doubled+e₁ being strongly ZSF does NOT immediately imply the Grail holds universally.
   - Gap between "one construction is strongly ZSF" and "shadow saturation is universal."

4. **Weak ZSF behavior**: Sets of length η(C_p^r) or beyond. Do they satisfy shadow saturation? Current proofs do not extend.

5. **Boundary cases (p=3, r=2)**: While p=3, r=4 was checked, the smallest cases remain fragile. Exhaustive verification at p=3, r=2 and p=3, r=3 is incomplete.
