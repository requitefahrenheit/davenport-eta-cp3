# Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Chevalley-Warning Cannot Prove Full Saturation**
CW only fires for p ≤ 5 (requires 4(p-1) < 17). While CW guarantees total tuple count ≡ 0 (mod p), the representation counts N(v) are non-uniform across targets at all tested primes, and weight constraints break the standard CW framework. Don't pursue CW as the main proof strategy.

## 2. **Fiber-(p-1) Bottleneck is Structurally Forced**
Exactly p³ vectors with first coordinate (p-1) are *only* reachable at weight p-1; this is a combinatorial consequence of the ZSF fiber structure (fiber-0 has 8 vectors with first coord 0, fiber-1 has 9 vectors with first coord 1), not an accident. These p³ vectors form a genuine saturation bottleneck that any proof must address directly—likely via a separate argument showing they're all covered despite minimal representation opportunities.

## 3. **Early Sumset Growth is Prime-Independent Below Weight 4**
Sumset expansion at weights 1–3 is identical across p=5 and p=7, suggesting the low-weight shadow is universal. This structural fact could enable an inductive proof: establish prime-independent saturation up to weight w₀, then handle the prime-dependent tail separately for p ≥ 7.
