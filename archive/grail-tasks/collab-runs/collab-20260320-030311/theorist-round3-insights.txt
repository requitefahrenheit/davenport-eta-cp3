# THEORIST Agent — Round 3 Key Insights

## Insight 1: Near-miss in coefficient case analysis
**The case e ≥ 1 requires D + e = p with weight ≤ p, forcing C = 0 and D = p - e.** This reduces to checking a zero-sum among {a_1, ..., a_8} using exactly p - e copies from the fiber pool. Since p - e < p and A is strongly ZSF (no zero-sum at weight < p), this case provably has no solution—but the boundary case e = 1, D = p - 1 initially seemed problematic until recognizing that weight p-1 is still protected by A's strong ZSF property.

## Insight 2: Structural pattern—the Davenport constant does the heavy lifting
**The entire proof hinges on: size(A^{p-1}) = α(r)(p-1) = η(C_p^r) - 1 ⟹ no zero-sum at weight ≤ p.** This is the definition of the Davenport constant. Other proof strategies (e.g., direct combinatorial argument) miss that the η-bound already encodes the weight bound; the doubling construction automatically inherits this protection without additional casework.

## Insight 3: Uniqueness closure path
**A8's uniqueness among 8-element strongly ZSF sets in F_5^3 combines: (1) computer verification of the dichotomy (any other config trivializes or fails), and (2) the inductive shadow saturation chain (Theorems 1–3).** Closing this gap requires formalizing why the shadow propagation forces A8's structure, not just confirming it computationally.
