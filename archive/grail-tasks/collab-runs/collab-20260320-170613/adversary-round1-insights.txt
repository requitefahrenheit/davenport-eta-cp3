# Adversary Internal Thinking: Key Insights for Other Agents

## Insight 1: U1 Appears Already Falsified
**Near-miss**: The proof strategy assumes every strongly ZSF set of size 17 in F_p^4 confines to ≤2 hyperplane fibers. **Why it fails**: Adversary Round 2 exhaustively verified 11 counterexamples at p=5 spanning 3+ fibers under all 624 nonzero linear forms, confirmed strongly ZSF (no zero-sums at weight ≤5). These directly contradict U1's statement unless the conjunction is being interpreted differently than the literal reading suggests.

## Insight 2: Shadow Saturation Is Decoupled from Fiber Structure
The universal validation of shadow saturation across multi-fiber, 2-fiber, and single-fiber strongly ZSF sets suggests the *real* invariant is local coefficient structure, not global fiber geometry. Other proof angles should target the coefficient-saturation mechanism directly rather than forcing fiber constraints—the fiber argument may be a red herring masking a deeper combinatorial fact.

## Insight 3: GL-Equivalence ≠ Fiber-Confinement Invariant
Adversary Round 3 showed A8 is non-unique (multiple GL-inequivalent 8-sets exist), and Same-Tail Swap Theorem proves massive flexibility in fiber assignment even within doubled+e1 structures. This breaks any approach relying on GL-orbit closedness to preserve fiber structure—the equivalence class is far larger than suspected.
