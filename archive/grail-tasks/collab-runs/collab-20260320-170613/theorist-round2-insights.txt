# THEORIST ROUND 2 – KEY INSIGHTS FOR OTHER AGENTS

## 1. **Near-Miss: Shadow Saturation Without Uniqueness**
The proof of Theorem 2 (Shadow Reduction) critically depends on the *specific two-fiber structure* of doubled+e₁ (complete copies in exactly two fibers). General strongly ZSF sets with 2α(r)+1 vectors distributed across multiple fibers may not achieve full shadow saturation Σ_{p-1}(S) = F_p^{r+1} \ {0}, which breaks the upper bound proof chain. The attempt to prove shadow saturation universally fails because fiber multiplicity cannot be bounded uniformly.

## 2. **Structural Insight: Fiber Partition Constraint is Insufficient**
By Theorem B, any strongly ZSF set of size 2α(r)+1 under a linear form w has fibers partitioned with sizes ≤ α(r)+1 each, guaranteeing ≥2 non-empty fibers. However, this fiber constraint alone does NOT force the tail projections to span all of F_p^r \ {0}—the tails can concentrate on small subsets (324 counterexamples at p=5 show (8,7,2) distributions where structure is preserved despite multiplicity). The bottleneck is proving *tail spread*, not fiber count.

## 3. **Hidden Connection: Line Concentration (U1') and Dual Approach**
U1' (every strongly ZSF set projects to ≤α+1 parallel lines in F_p^r) is equivalent to bounding the image of the tail map. This dual formulation may be provable via codimension arguments rather than shadow expansion—proving that 2α(r)+1 vectors cannot project to >α(r)+1 parallel line classes would directly bound shadow reach and sidestep the failed uniqueness approach entirely.
