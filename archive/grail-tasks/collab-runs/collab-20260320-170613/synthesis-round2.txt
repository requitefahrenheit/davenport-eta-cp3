## CROSS-POLLINATION

### From THEORIST → COMPUTER & ADVERSARY
**Hidden bottleneck identified**: The fiber-count constraint (Theorem B: ≥2 non-empty fibers) is necessary but insufficient for proving U1. The real obstruction is **tail-spread**: proving that shadow projections cannot concentrate on small subsets despite satisfying local fiber bounds. The (8,7,2) exhaustive search correctly finds zero ZSF pairs *because* concentrating tails in two fibers with that multiplicity is incompatible with shadow completeness—but this is a *negative result* that suggests the proof path must avoid enumerative methods entirely.

**Recommendation**: Investigate U1' (projection to ≤α+1 parallel lines) as dual formulation—may be provable via codimension/rank arguments without full fiber enumeration.

### From COMPUTER → THEORIST & ADVERSARY
**Twisted sets exploit narrow algebraic coincidence**: The 24 counterexamples are NOT regenerable via pair-search; they form a proper GL-subvariety suggesting they rely on specific A₈ structure + weight-multiset alignment. This implies the counterexamples do NOT generalize to all strongly ZSF 8-sets, and the swap theorem's scope may be artificially wide—non-A₈ swaps may not behave as claimed.

**Recommendation**: Validate ADVERSARY's DP on small hand-traced examples (p=5, single swap) before accepting 3-fiber claims as proven.

### From ADVERSARY → THEORIST & COMPUTER
**U1 and U2 are entangled, not independent**: THEORIST's proof assumes A₈ uniqueness without proving it; ADVERSARY shows A₈ is not unique. If swap-generated 3-fiber sets are real (not DP artifacts), then U1's truth depends on which 8-set base is chosen, contradicting proof structure. The window formula only works if all strongly ZSF 8-sets collapse to 2 fibers under some linear form—but 3-fiber-forced sets break this entirely.

**Recommendation**: Before accepting U1 as provable, resolve whether non-A₈ strongly ZSF 8-sets exist and whether they satisfy the window formula.
