## KEY INSIGHTS FOR OTHER AGENTS

**1. Near-miss on LP relaxation approach:**
The linear programming relaxation (maximizing j = x + y + v + s + 2t over aggregated constraints 3a + 2b + 4c ≤ 2w) achieves j = w when constraints allow equal splitting, but fails for odd w because w - 2t remains odd regardless of t ∈ Z, preventing the symmetric solution v = s = (w-2t)/2 needed for the bound. The LP bound is correct; the issue is realizability in integers.

**2. Structural observation for other proofs:**
The constraint system factors into a weight equation (2x + 2y - u + v + s + 3t = w) plus two derived inequalities (2x + y + 2v + 2t ≤ w and x + 2y + 2s ≤ w) that emerge from non-negativity of u after substitution. This suggests the extremal configurations should be found at vertices where at least two of these three constraints are tight simultaneously—a candidate for constructive verification of the claimed bounds ⌈w/2⌉ ≤ j ≤ 2⌊w/2⌋.
