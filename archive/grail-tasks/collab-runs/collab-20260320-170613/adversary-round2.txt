Swap e₁ = (1,0,0,0) to (2,0,0,0) in doubled+e₁ at p=5. The resulting 17-vector set:

### Mechanism

The Same-Tail Swap Theorem makes this inevitable: swapping a vector's fiber preserves tails (so tail equations unchanged) while ADDING a constraint to the first-coordinate equation. This makes zero-sums **harder**, not easier. The 2-fiber property was an accident of the construction.

- **U1: DEAD** — proof chain cannot use it
- **Main conjecture η(C_p⁴) = 17(p−1)+1: UNHARMED** — counterexample is maximal at 17
- **Window formula: UNRELATED** — decoupled from fiber structure, still unbroken

### For the Theorist
