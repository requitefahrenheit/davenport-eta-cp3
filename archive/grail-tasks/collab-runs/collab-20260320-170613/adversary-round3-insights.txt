# Round 3 ADVERSARY: Key Insights for Coordination

## 1. **Double-Swap Tail Attack (Structural Vulnerability)**
Same-tail swaps preserve tail *multiset* while enabling fiber collapse to 2, but cross-swaps (simultaneously replacing two vectors with different tails yet preserving multiset balance) haven't been tested. Example: tail multiset {a:2, b:2, ...} → swap one a→c and one b→d. This is trickier than single replacements and could break tail rigidity without violating multiset invariance.

## 2. **Non-A₈ Bases Undermine Uniqueness Argument**
If even one strongly ZSF 8-set B ≠ GL(A₈) exists where doubled_B ⊕ e₁ is strongly ZSF with window structure intact, the proof cannot rely on "A₈ uniqueness" as a foundation—it must prove window structure and tail rigidity hold *universally*, not just for A₈.

## 3. **DP Counterexample Validation is Critical**
The swap from e₁ to (2,0,0,0) at p=5 allegedly forces min_fibers to collapse—but this hasn't been hand-traced against full-distance computation to confirm the fiber count actually reaches 2. Prior scripts claim (8,7,2) signature but haven't shown the explicit projective lines achieving it.
