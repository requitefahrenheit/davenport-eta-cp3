# Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. Near-Miss: Fiber Projection Approach Fails on Weight Coupling
The inductive strategy of partitioning vectors by a linear form w and applying induction to ker(w) breaks because: when vectors in fiber F_c admit a zero-sum with weight W < p in the projection, the lift gives last coordinate Wc ≠ 0 mod p. This forces W = p exactly, but the inductive hypothesis provides weight ≤ p, not weight = p. **The weight constraint doesn't decouple across fibers.**

## 2. Structural Observation: The Zero Fiber is Special
Vectors in F_0 = ker(w) already satisfy the last-coordinate constraint trivially. If |F_0| ≥ α(r-1) + 1, induction applies directly with preserved non-proportionality. When F_0 is small, vectors spread to nonzero fibers, forcing a balance problem: combining partial zero-sums from F_c and F_c' requires solving Wc + W'c' ≡ 0 (mod p) simultaneously with weight bounds—this couples the fiber contributions in a non-decomposable way.

## 3. Unexpected Connection: Recursive Formula Suggests Extremal Structure Matters More Than Generic Counting
The bound α(r) = 2α(r-1) + 1 (not α(r) = 2α(r-1)) hints that the extremal set has a specific geometric form (two copies on parallel hyperplanes plus one pivot vector). **Direct counting arguments may be insufficient; the proof may require reconstructing why this particular multiplicative structure is forced, not just verifying it on generic vector families.**
