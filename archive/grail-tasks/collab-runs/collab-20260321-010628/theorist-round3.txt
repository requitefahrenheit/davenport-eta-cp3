### 2. The Excess Criterion (NEW — proved)

**THEOREM (Excess Criterion).** With notation above, define the *excess* $E = \sum_{j \text{ active}} \tilde{c}_j(\tilde{a}_j - 1)$. If all active vectors satisfy $\tilde{a}_j \geq 1$ (equivalently, $w(v_j) \neq 0$ for the coordinate form $w = e_1^*$), then:

**(a)** $E \geq 0$.

**(b)** If $W_q + E < p$ (no modular wrap-around), then weight $= p - E \leq p$. In particular, weight $< p$ when $E > 0$.

**(c)** If $W_q + E \geq p$, then weight $= 2p - E$. Weight $\leq p$ iff $E \geq p$.

*Proof.* (a) Each $\tilde{a}_j \in \{1,\ldots,p-1\}$ gives $\tilde{a}_j - 1 \geq 0$, and $\tilde{c}_j \geq 0$.

(c) $\tilde{\mu} = W_q + E - p$. Weight $= p - (W_q + E - p) + W_q = 2p - E$. $\square$

**Corollary.** The projection gives weight $\leq p$ whenever either:
- No modular wrap-around occurs ($W_q + E < p$), OR
