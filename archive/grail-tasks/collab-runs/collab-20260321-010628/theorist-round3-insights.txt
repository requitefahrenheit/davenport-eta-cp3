# Key Insights from Mathematician's Internal Thinking

## 1. Near-miss: Projection weight-coefficient coupling
The inductive lifting step breaks because we can't simultaneously control (a) the zero-sum weight Σcᵢ ∈ [1,p] in the quotient and (b) the lift coefficient μ: when μ > aₖ, the recovered weight jumps to 2p instead of staying ≤ p. The bottleneck is μ-dependent—when μ is small, it forces other coefficients to vanish, contradicting non-proportionality of projected vectors. This is not a fixable gap but reveals the projection strategy as fundamentally constrained by the interaction between quotient structure and weight recovery.

## 2. Structural observation: Davenport constant vs. η-invariant
The recursion s(Cₚʳ) = 2s(Cₚʳ) - 1 (where s(G) = η(G) + exp(G) - 1) gives cleaner induction than attacking η directly. The formula α(r+1) = 2α(r) + 1 matches this s-recursion exactly, suggesting the true path forward uses s rather than η—this sidesteps the weight-recovery obstruction entirely by working with a derived invariant that respects the doubling structure.

## 3. Multi-fiber cap-evasion pattern
Vectors distributed across ≥3 fibers can form cap configurations that evade existing zero-sum mechanisms because collinear triple avoidance is not forced by fiber structure alone. The gap suggests attacking via Partition Dichotomy (Theorem 1(c)): force all but α(r) vectors into ≤2 fibers, then apply two-fiber bounds. This reframes the problem from "find zero-sums directly" to "control the span of the non-proportional set."
