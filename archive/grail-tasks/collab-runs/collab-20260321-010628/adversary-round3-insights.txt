# Adversary Internal Reasoning: Three Critical Findings

## 1. **Projection Non-Proportionality Collapse (Near-miss)**
The fiber concentration lemma assumes projecting 9 non-proportional vectors (c,u₁),...,(c,u₉) from a single fiber onto ker(w) preserves non-proportionality. **This fails**: vectors (c,u) and (c,λu) are non-proportional in F_p⁴ but project to proportional vectors u, λu in F_p³. The inductive hypothesis—that 9 non-proportional vectors guarantee a zero-sum—cannot apply directly to the *projective equivalence classes* of the tail components, which can collapse dramatically below 9 classes.

## 2. **Forbidden Directions Constraint is Tight but Fragile**
Avoiding zero-projections requires u ∉ S and bounds bad choices by C(9,2)=36 forbidden directions. This works for p≥7, but the constraint is *exactly* the fiber size squared. For p close to 7 the argument becomes brittle, and small perturbations in the collinear triple mechanism could break the counting margin entirely—no room for error in Theorem D or the weight realization conjecture.

## 3. **Integer Weight Realization Gap is Structural, Not Quantitative**
IWRC is flagged as the remaining gap: mod-p surjectivity (Theorem D) guarantees a weight-p solution *modulo p*, but integer weights could be w+kp for any k. This isn't a small technical hole—it reflects that the mod-p induction **decouples from the integer lattice structure**. No cardinality or projection argument can recover this lost information without additional constraints on the zero-sum's structure (e.g., enforcing w itself is optimal, not just w mod p).
