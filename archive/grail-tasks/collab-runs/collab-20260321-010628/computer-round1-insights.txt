# Key Insights from Computational Analysis

## 1. **CW Congruences Cannot Force Non-Zero Solutions**
The Chevalley-Warning congruence approach (attempting to show N_p > 0 for rank-4 systems) hits a structural wall: the constraint system is always consistent mod p, so congruence arguments alone cannot rule out N_p = 0. **This means the proof must go through explicit zero-sum structure, not number-theoretic forcing.** The two-fiber reduction works because it exploits concrete fiber geometry; any multi-fiber argument must do likewise.

## 2. **The 94 Residual Cases at p=5 Reveal a Support-4 Bottleneck**
Of 607 candidate 18th vectors, 513 fall to support-3 zero-sums, leaving 94 survivors that require support-4 four-element zero-sums to eliminate. **This near-miss suggests the bound 17(p−1)+1 is tight not by accident but by a structural balance**: the doubled-fiber construction S17 uses exactly the projective points needed to avoid dense support-4 dependencies. A proof strategy should exploit this: show that any 18th vector *must* interact with S17 in a way that forces a low-weight zero-sum, rather than proving such zero-sums exist abstractly.

## 3. **Uniqueness of S17 (doubled+e₁) as a Maximal ZSF Set is Unproven**
Computation has verified S17 works for p∈{5,7} but hasn't ruled out other maximal size-17 ZSF sets. **If S17 is not unique, the proof strategy shifts**: either (a) all maximal ZSF sets of size 17 are rigid under GL(4,p) (unlikely), or (b) the proof must be *uniform* across all such sets (showing any extension forces a zero-sum), not tailored to S17's geometry. A quick exhaustive search for alternative size-17 ZSF sets at p=5 would disambiguate.
