## ADVERSARY ANALYSIS

### Attack 1: The Support-4 Weight Obstruction (NEW FINDING)

The dependency `(1,2,3,4)` in F_5 produces weight `2p = 10` for ALL rescalings μ. This is because `{1,2,3,4} = F_5*`, and rescaling by μ just permutes the terms, preserving the sum.

More generally: any k-element dependency whose coefficients form **antipodal pairs** `{a, p-a}` gives constant weight `kp/2` for all μ. With k=4 and two antipodal pairs, weight is always `2p > p`.

**Implication**: Support-4 zero-sums CANNOT be guaranteed from individual linear dependencies. The dependency `(1,1,4,4)` at p=5 also gives constant weight 10 (since `{1,4}={1,-1}` and each pair contributes weight p).

**Contrast with support 3**: For 3-element dependencies with Σaᵢ ≡ 0 mod p, the μ↔(p-μ) symmetry gives `w_μ + w_{p-μ} = 3p`. Since weights are in `{p, 2p}`, exactly `(p-1)/2` values give weight p. Weight p is ALWAYS achievable at support 3.

**For support ≥ 4**: `w_μ + w_{p-μ} = kp` allows a fixed point at `kp/2` (when k is even). ALL μ can give weight `kp/2`, making weight ≤ p impossible from that specific dependency.

This means any proof of the grail must exploit **global structure across multiple dependencies**, not just individual ones.

### Attack 2: Probing the Uniqueness Gap

The Cortex shows the entire grail now reduces to: **doubled+e1 is the unique strongly ZSF set of size 2α(r)+1**.

### Attack 3: Circularity Check on Shadow Saturation

The shadow saturation proof uses:

> "If η(C_p^r) = α(r)(p-1)+1 and A is a maximal ZSF set of size α(r), then Σ_{p-1}(A) = F_p^r \ {0}."

This requires the **exact value** of η at rank r. At rank 3, η is PROVED, so shadow saturation at rank 3 is unconditional. At rank 4+, η is what we're proving — so the induction needs uniqueness at rank r+1, not shadow saturation at rank r+1.

**Verified: no circularity.** The chain is:
- η(C_p^3) proved → shadow sat at rank 3 → shadow sat for doubled+e1 in rank 4 (via lifting Theorem 2) → projection strategy at rank 4 → if uniqueness holds, η(C_p^4) proved.

### Attack 4: Can Uniqueness at Rank r Imply Uniqueness at Rank r+1?

If this were true, the entire grail would follow from uniqueness of A8 at rank 3.

**Obstruction**: A maximal ZSF (2α(r)+1)-set in F_p^{r+1} need not have the doubled+e1 fiber structure. It could spread across 3+ fibers with ≤ 8 directions each. The within-fiber η bound requires α(r)+1 = 9 directions per fiber (for fiber 0) or s-bound needs even more (for nonzero fibers). With 17 directions across 3+ fibers, no fiber reaches threshold.

**But**: at larger p, the rank-3 theorem handles more collinear triples (77% non-degenerate at p=13, approaching 100%). A multi-fiber ZSF set must avoid ALL non-degenerate triples, which becomes exponentially unlikely as p grows. This suggests uniqueness is **easier to prove for large p**, with p=5 being the hardest case.

### Attack 5: The Projection Asymmetry

The Cortex shows: projecting out v₁₈ works 100% at p=5,7, but projecting out other vectors fails often (fiber-0 vectors: ~12% success). This means the proof MUST choose v_k = v₁₈ (the new vector), not an arbitrary vector.

How many projections become 0? At most 1 (since v₁₈ determines a unique projective point, and S₁₇ has no proportional pairs). So ≥ 16 nonzero projections remain, well above the threshold of 9.

**This checks out.** The projection strategy with v_k = v₁₈ is sound.
