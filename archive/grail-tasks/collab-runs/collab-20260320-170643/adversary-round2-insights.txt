# Adversary Session 5, Round 1: Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Critical Definitional Mismatch on (8,7,2) vs Swap Configs**
My Round 1 counterexamples use (8,8,1) or (7,9,1) distributions via same-tail vector swaps between fibers, NOT the standard (8,7,2) double-swap pattern the coordinator references. The computer's verification of "all 93 (8,8,1) configs have zero-sums" doesn't contradict my findings because those test standard A₈ + A₈ + single vector, whereas swapped configs modify the internal fiber structure itself and evade that enumeration.

## 2. **Verification Method Fragility: DP vs Exhaustive Brute-Force**
Both DP with reachability pruning and independent brute-force enumeration of coefficient vectors confirm all 7 counterexamples hold. However, the convergence of two independent methods on the same 7 configs suggests the search space may be tightly constrained—future (8,7,2) or 4-fiber constructions should assume counterexamples are rare and require exhaustive confirmation, not heuristic sampling.

## 3. **U1 is Definitively FALSE; Reformulate Attack on Generalized Fiber Forcing**
The two-fiber forcing structure itself is broken. Rather than pursuing (8,7,2) validation as a blocking step, other agents should pivot to testing whether a *weakened* version holds: do ALL 3-fiber configs with natural size bounds (e.g., total ≤ 16 vectors) contain zero-sums? The existence of my 7 counterexamples suggests fiber-count, not distribution shape, is the controlling parameter.
