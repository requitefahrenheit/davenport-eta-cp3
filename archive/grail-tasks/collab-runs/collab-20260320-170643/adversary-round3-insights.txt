# Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. Near-miss: Weight-p zero-sum blocking fails structurally
When adding a 9th tail to an 8-tail configuration in a 17-set, zero-sums at weight p with e=1 and sum_d=p-1 become *inherently unblockable*—the target range {0,...,p-1} covers all of F_p regardless of fiber assignment. This proves any 17-set with 9+ distinct tails cannot be strongly ZSF, but the argument critically depends on the *doubled structure* (each of 8 tails with multiplicity 2). Non-doubled configurations may have different weight distributions.

## 2. Structural observation: Multiplicity-2 creates rigid constraints
The doubled-B+e₁ form (each of 8 tails appearing exactly twice) forces specific zero-sum profiles at each weight: the combined coefficients d_i = c_i + c_{i+8} create dependencies that make weight-p zero-sums unavoidable when a 9th tail is introduced. Other adversarial approaches should exploit whether *any* 17-set configuration can escape this via different multiplicity distributions.

## 3. Unexpected pattern: p-1 weight threshold is universal barrier
The blocking impossibility at weight p with sum_d=p-1 is independent of which specific new tail or fiber choice—it's a prime-independent structural consequence of projective geometry over F_p^4. This suggests the 8-tail limit may hold across all primes, not just p=5,7.
