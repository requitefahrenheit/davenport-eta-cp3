# Adversary Internal Thinking: Key Insights for Collaborators

## 1. **Sumset Restriction Pitfall (Structural)**
The sumset completeness claim Σ_k(A₈) + Σ_l(A₈) = Σ_{k+l}(A₈) is deceptively easy to break *if tested on the restricted line L alone*, but this is a false lead—most L-point sums escape to non-L vectors in F_p³. The real claim requires verification in full 3D space, not the line projection; **restriction to invariant structures breaks additive closure**.

## 2. **Window Formula Hardness (Near-Miss)**
Exhaustively verified the window formula j ∈ ⌈w/2⌉ to 2⌊w/2⌋ across p = 5,7,11,13,17,19,23 in the p-independent regime (p ≥ 2w+1) and **found zero counterexamples**—this suggests the formula may be provably tight rather than a heuristic, not an empirical near-miss but a structural necessity.

## 3. **3-Fiber Configuration Gap (Unexpected Connection)**
The (8,7,2) attack at p=5 produced two candidate survivors that pass individual-vector checks but require full DP validation across *combined extra vectors*—**the bottleneck is whether simultaneous use of multiple removed vectors can create undetected zero-sums**; this suggests the strongly-ZSF property has hidden polynomial-degree dependencies, not just linear constraints.
