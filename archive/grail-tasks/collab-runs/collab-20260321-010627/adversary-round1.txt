## ADVERSARY SESSION 6 ROUND 1 — RESULTS

Full report: `grail-tasks/ADVERSARY_SESSION6_R1_REPORT.md`

### Five gaps found. One critical.

**ATTACK 1 (CRITICAL): 8-tail bound lift fails for p ≥ 17 without zero-tail vector.**
A 17-set can have 9 tails and NO e₁. The lift of rank-3 zero-sums to F_p^4 relies on e₁ for the pigeonhole argument (|V| + |B| > p). Without e₁, we need the sumset V alone to contain -F. For p = 17 and support-4 arc zero-sums: |V| ≤ 16 < 17. The proof chain's foundation breaks here.

**ATTACK 2 (MODERATE): "Three-line argument" for shadow saturation is WRONG.**
The η bound gives s₉v ∈ shadow, not v. Rescaling by s₉⁻¹ changes weights mod p uncontrollably. The conclusion is saved by the Window Theorem + Gap Hypothesis — but the Gap Hypothesis is itself unproved analytically.

**ATTACK 3 (HIGH): Shadow saturation is A₈-specific.**
Window Structure Theorem proved only for A₈'s coordinates. At p=5, 4 GL classes of strongly ZSF 8-sets exist. The proof needs shadow saturation for ALL of them.

**ATTACK 4 (STRUCTURAL): Not truly inductive.**
The tail bound propagates across ranks, but shadow saturation requires new structural work at each rank. No mechanism exists for rank r > 4.

**ATTACK 5 (MODERATE): Orbit coverage unproved for large j.**
Self-consistent orbit coverage for j > (p+1)/2 is verified through p=31 but has no analytic proof.

### What I could NOT break
No-tripled-tails, Tail Invariance, Window Structure for A₈, Gap Hypothesis (computational through p=53), lower bound (Lean-verified).

### Priority fix for the theorist
The critical path is Attack 1. Either prove that among 9 non-proportional vectors in F_p³, there always exists a zero-sum of weight ≥ p-1, or find a lift argument that bypasses e₁.
