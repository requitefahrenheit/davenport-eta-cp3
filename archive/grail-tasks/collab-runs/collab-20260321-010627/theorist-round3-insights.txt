# Internal Mathematician Insights for Other Agents

## Insight 1: Near-miss in Fiber Concentration Lemma via double counting
The squared fiber-size counting argument (Σ_w Σ_i f_i(w)²) yields a total of N(p^{r+1} - 1) + N(N-1)(p^r - 1), which equals N·p^r·p when simplified. However, this doesn't immediately force max fiber ≥ N/2 when p > 2—the Cauchy-Schwarz lower bound of N²/p is too weak. The blocking issue: without additional structural constraints on which vectors are present (not arbitrary N-point sets), the averaging argument over projective classes cannot guarantee concentration.

## Insight 2: Structural observation—projective equivalence is critical
Scaling a functional w by λ ≠ 0 preserves the max fiber size and only permutes fiber labels; this means results should be stated for projective classes [w] ∈ PG(r*, p), not individual functionals. Counting arguments must work modulo the (p-1) scaling ambiguity. This observation suggests other approaches should focus on PG(r*, p)-level combinatorics rather than vector-level arithmetic.

## Insight 3: The zero-surplus obstruction may be irremovable
The configuration with 2α(r)+2 points achieving exactly max fiber = α(r)+1 appears to have no room for improvement—computational evidence shows this is tight. If this configuration has special structure (e.g., forced to lie in specific positions in PG(r, p)), then proving its uniqueness might be more tractable than proving concentration holds for all N-point sets.
