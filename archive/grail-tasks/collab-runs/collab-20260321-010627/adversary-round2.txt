# ADVERSARY ROUND 2 REPORT

## Five Attacks — Three Things Broken

### ATTACK 1: Weight-p Barrier Theorem (PROVED)

**Theorem.** Any zero-sum within a single non-kernel fiber has weight ≡ 0 (mod p), minimum weight = p.

*Proof:* Vectors in fiber c have first coord c. Zero-sum forces c·(Σaᵢ) = 0 ⟹ Σaᵢ ≡ 0 (mod p) ⟹ weight ≥ p. □

**Corollary.** Cross-fiber triples on fibers (0, 1, c) have weight ∈ {p, 2p} — **always**, for all p, all c. Proved algebraically, verified computationally at p = 5, 7, 11, 13.

### ATTACK 2: p=5 Total Rigidity (COMPUTED — NEW)

At p=5, **ALL** cross-fiber collinear triples have weight ≥ p, including all-nonzero fiber triples:

| Fiber triple | Min weight | Status |
|---|---|---|
| (1,2,3) | 5 = p | No sub-p zero-sum |
| (1,2,4) | 5 = p | No sub-p zero-sum |
| (1,3,4) | 5 = p | No sub-p zero-sum |
| (2,3,4) | 5 = p | No sub-p zero-sum |

**Why:** No three distinct nonzero elements of F₅ sum to 0 (sums are 1, 2, 3, 4 mod 5). So (1,1,1)-coefficient triples never work. At p ≥ 7 this changes: **100% of all-nonzero fiber triples have weight < p** (min = 3 at p=7,11,13).

### ATTACK 3: Modular Window is SPARSE (COMPUTED — Coordinator directive resolved)

The constraint w·j mod p ∈ [⌈w/2⌉, 2⌊w/2⌋] for j ∈ {1,...,p-2}:

| p | Density | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 41.7% | < 50% |
| 7 | 36.7% | < 50% |
| 11 | **31.1%** | < 50% |
| 13 | **30.3%** | < 50% |
| 17 | 28.7% | < 50% |
| 29 | 27.1% | < 50% |
| 31 | 27.0% | < 50% |

**Dense coverage claim is FALSE.** Density decreases monotonically toward ~27%. Weight w = p always gives 0% (since pj ≡ 0 ∉ window). Note: the *window structure* on Σ_w(A8) ∩ L is still valid — that's an additive sumset property, not a multiplicative orbit claim.

### ATTACK 4: Weight Flexibility Architecture is DEAD at p=5

The theorist's architecture — "Weight Flexibility + Cauchy-Davenport to combine fiber zero-sums" — **cannot work**:

1. **Non-kernel intra-fiber:** weight ≡ 0 (mod p), min = p. Two fibers: ≥ 2p > p. **Dead.**
2. **Fiber-0 cross-fiber:** weight ∈ {p, 2p}. Not combinable below p. **Dead.**
3. **All-nonzero cross-fiber at p=5:** weight ≥ p (Attack 2). **Dead.**
4. **Pigeonhole:** With ≥ 3 fibers at any rank r ≥ 4, max fiber ≤ ⌈(α(r)+1)/3⌉ < α(r-1)+1. No fiber reaches inductive threshold.

At p ≥ 7, the architecture *might* be rescuable via all-nonzero fiber triples (weight < p available). But p=5 is a hard wall.

### ATTACK 5: Fiber Arithmetic Confirms Universal Gap

For ALL ranks r = 4 through 8: induction fires only with ≤ 2 fibers. With ≥ 3 fibers, NO fiber reaches the inductive threshold. The gap is structural and rank-independent.

## What Survives

1. **Shadow saturation** — universal for all tested ZSF sets (0 counterexamples), but unproved analytically for non-doubled+e1 configurations. This remains the most promising path.

2. **Direct cross-fiber zero-sums at weight exactly p** — the dominant kill mechanism (70% at p=5, 67% at p=7). The proof should target weight = p, not weight < p.

3. **The s-constant connection** — s(C_p^r) = η(C_p^r) + p - 1. The proof should show that α(r)+1 non-proportional directions force a zero-sum of weight **exactly** p, not just ≤ p.

## Directives for THEORIST

1. **Abandon Weight Flexibility + Cauchy-Davenport.** It's dead at p=5 and fragile elsewhere.
2. **Prove shadow saturation for general ZSF sets.** This is the single remaining gap.
3. **If pursuing fiber induction, target weight = p exactly** (s-constant, not η). The sub-p contribution is a mirage at small p.
4. **Consider a p=5-specific proof** as a base case, then a separate argument for p ≥ 7 where cross-fiber weight < p is available.
