# COMPUTER AGENT: Key Insights from Internal Exploration

## 1. **Counting Ambiguity Resolved, But Verification Strategy Clarified**
The "607 possible 18th vectors" for p=5, rank 4 likely means: 624 nonzero vectors minus the 17 already in the construction. However, checking all 607 requires handling proportionality carefully—roughly 68 vectors are proportional to the construction (17 vectors × 4 scalar multiples each), leaving ~556 genuinely new directions to test. The exhaustive verification is computationally feasible but the categorization (how many candidates are eliminated by support-3 vs support-4 vs support-5 zero-sums) needs systematic implementation.

## 2. **Critical Structural Dependency: Weighted Zero-Sum Structure by Support Size**
For p=5, rank 4, the possible (support, weight) constraints that bind ZSF-ness are: support-3 with weights {3,4,5}, support-4 with weights {4,5}, and support-5 with weight 5. The support-3 case subdivides further (e.g., v₁+v₂+v₃=0 vs 2v₁+v₂+v₃=0), and most elimination likely concentrates there since collinear triples are dense. If the 17-vector construction resists all these dependencies by design (not accident), the fiber structure or its symmetry must actively prevent support-3 collinear triples—this should be verifiable and might explain why p=7 with the same construction also works.

## 3. **Conjecture Test Point: p=7 Extension is Tractable but Ambiguous**
Testing η(C₇⁴) requires 103 copies of a rank-4 point set and checking whether 18 non-proportional vectors *always* contain a weighted zero-sum (weight ≤7). The candidate space (~10,000 vectors after removing proportionals) is larger than p=5 but still manageable. However, **success here is necessary but not sufficient for the conjecture**—proving uniqueness of the 17-vector construction or showing the formula holds for all p requires either pattern recognition across multiple primes or a structural proof that the fiber method is optimal.
