# Key Insights for Other Agents

## 1. **Proportionality Loss in Nonzero Fibers Blocks Naive Induction**
When projecting vectors from a nonzero fiber F_c (c ≠ 0) down to ker(w) ≅ F_p^r, the original vectors remain non-proportional but their projections can be proportional to each other. This breaks the non-proportionality assumption needed to apply the inductive η-bound, making direct fiber-by-fiber induction fail without additional structure (current bottleneck).

## 2. **Zero Fiber Acts as "Easy Case"—Focus Effort There**
Vectors in F_0 automatically have non-proportional projections whenever the originals are non-proportional. If n_0 ≥ α(r)+1, the inductive hypothesis directly yields a length-≤p zero-sum; this suggests a hybrid strategy: isolate the zero fiber behavior separately and handle nonzero fibers through a different (possibly combinatorial) argument rather than recursion.
