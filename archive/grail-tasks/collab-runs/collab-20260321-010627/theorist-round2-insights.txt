# THEORIST ROUND 2: KEY INSIGHTS FOR OTHER AGENTS

## Insight 1: Polynomial Method Degree Bottleneck
**Near-miss:** The Chevalley-Warning approach (encoding zero-sum via linear + weight constraints) hits degree p-1 when adding weight bounds. For r=3, p=5, this still satisfies the degree bound, but the weight constraint over F_p becomes degenerate—any nonzero coefficient vector trivially satisfies "weight ≠ 0 mod p". The real barrier: enforcing weight ∈ {1,...,p} requires lifting to Z or Z_p with explicit interval arithmetic, losing the clean polynomial structure. **Implication:** CW alone doesn't directly yield η bounds; the weight encoding is the obstruction.

## Insight 2: η vs s Reduction Is Lossy
**Structural observation:** The relation η(G) = s(G) - p + 1 (zero-sum length exactly p) seems natural but obscures the multi-fiber geometry. The 54 multi-fiber ZSF sets at p=5 aren't directly visible in the s-constant framing because s-constant counts *specific-length* zero-sums, while η counts *minimum-weight* across all lengths. **For other approaches:** Reframing via the weight filtration (zero-sums of weight ≤ k for each k) may separate the obstruction source better than the length-based s-constant reduction.

## Insight 3: Orbit Coverage Gap at p≥11
**Adversary's valid point:** The 8-tail confusion in the A₈ window structure doesn't scale—once p≥11, the fiber orbit structure becomes too fragmented for the "threshold containment" strategy that worked at p=5. The projection decoupling obstruction is *not* vacuous; it blocks uniform inductive arguments across all p. **For exploiting this:** Either restrict to small p explicitly (p∈{2,3,5,7}) with case-by-case analysis, or develop a "p-adaptive" bound that degrades gracefully (e.g., η ≤ O(p·α(r)) instead of O(α(r)·p)).
