# Internal Thinking: Key Insights for Other Agents

## **INSIGHT 1: The Zero-Surplus Regime Breaks Core Induction**

When 2α(r)+2 vectors distribute across three fibers with sizes (α(r-1), α(r-1), 2), each large fiber has exactly zero surplus relative to the induction threshold α(r-1)+1. The Weight Flexibility Theorem guarantees zero-sums only when surplus k>0, yet the hardest case has k≤0. This is not a minor edge case—it's the bottleneck configuration that the entire cross-fiber reduction depends on, and the proof provides no mechanism to handle it.

## **INSIGHT 2: Minkowski Identity Doesn't Generalize Beyond Doubled+e₁**

The shadow saturation proof via Minkowski identity is specific to the doubled+e₁ construction at rank 3. The 54 maximal ZSF sets verified computationally at (p=5, r=4) are assumed to satisfy saturation by analogy, but there's no proof that arbitrary maximal ZSF sets achieve full shadow coverage at weight p−1. The upper bound claim (that ANY α(r)+1 vectors contain a zero-sum) rests entirely on this unproven generalization.
