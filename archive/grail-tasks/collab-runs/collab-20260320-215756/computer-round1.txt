## NEW DISCOVERY: Weight Interval Theorem

For 1 ≤ j ≤ (p-1)/2, the vector (0,j,j) is in the **exact-weight-w** sumset Σ_w(A8) **if and only if**:

$$2\lceil j/2 \rceil \leq w \leq 2j$$

The interval is **contiguous**. Verified for all p = 5,...,43.

**The gap follows immediately**: (0,j,j) ∉ Σ_{p-1} iff max_weight = 2j < p-1, iff j ≤ (p-3)/2.

## Structural Analysis

**Coordinate-sum partition**: A8 splits as {a₁...a₄} (coord sum 1) and {a₅...a₈} (coord sum 2). For (0,j,j) at weight w=p-1:
- **Primary branch** T₁ = 2(p-1-j), T₂ = 2j-(p-1) < 0. **Infeasible** (negative T₂).
- **Secondary branch** T₁ = p-2-2j, T₂ = 2j+1. **Partition exists**, but the within-fiber linear system over 8 coefficient variables has **no solution**. Brute-force confirmed at p=5,7,13,29,43.

**|Σ_w| p-independence**: Sizes 1, 8, 30, 77 for w=0,1,2,3 are **identical** across all p ≥ 11. Stabilizes at 159 for w=4.

**Pairwise sumset saturation** (Conj 4): |Σ(A8,w₀) + Σ(A8,w₁)| = p³ whenever w₀+w₁ ≥ p. **Verified** at p=5,7,11. Threshold is sharp: at w₀+w₁ = p-1, coverage is partial.

## Proof Strategy for THEORIST

The gap hypothesis reduces to two parts:
1. **(Constructive)** Show (0,j,j) ∈ Σ_w for w ∈ [2⌈j/2⌉, 2j]. Likely doable — explicit coefficient assignments exist.
2. **(Infeasibility)** Show (0,j,j) ∉ Σ_w for w > 2j. This is the hard part. At the secondary branch (T₁=p-2-2j, T₂=2j+1), the fiber-2 constraint requires s₅+s₇ and s₆+s₈ to simultaneously satisfy two independent mod-p equations with total s₅+s₆+s₇+s₈ = 2j+1 < p. The system has **exactly 1 too few degrees of freedom**.
